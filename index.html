App information

What the app is, why it matters, and how it helps.

This section mirrors the spirit of the in-app info area — giving users a clear explanation of why added sugar awareness matters, what counts as added sugar, and what each feature is designed to do.

What counts as added sugar?

Added sugar means sugar that is added during processing, preparation, or serving — not the natural sugar found inside whole fruit, plain milk, or other unsweetened whole foods.

  • Examples include table sugar, syrups, sweeteners in soda, sweet coffee drinks, desserts, candy, flavored yogurt, pastries, sauces, cereals, and many packaged snacks.
  • The app is focused on added sugar only, so users can track the part of sugar intake that is most hidden in everyday products.
  • This keeps the experience simpler and more actionable than tracking every type of sugar.

Why is juice often treated like added sugar behaviorally?

Even when juice contains naturally derived sugar, it can behave more like a sugary product in real life because much of the fruit’s original fiber structure is removed and the sugar becomes easier to consume quickly.

  • People can drink a large amount of sugar from juice faster than they would eat the equivalent whole fruit.
  • Juice often feels healthy, so it is easy to underestimate how much sugar it contributes to the day.
  • That is why knowsugar helps users pay attention to sweet drinks and juice-like products as part of better daily awareness.

Why track added sugar?

Added sugar can build up quietly through drinks, snacks, desserts, sauces, and packaged foods. knowsugar helps make that invisible number visible, so users can build awareness and make better daily choices.

  • Focus on added sugar only, not naturally occurring sugars from foods like fruit or milk.
  • Spot repeat sources that push intake higher than expected.
  • Use simple daily tracking as a practical behavior-change tool.

Personalized added sugar limit

The app provides a personalized daily added sugar limit to give users a clear upper boundary. The goal is not perfection — it is awareness, consistency, and a practical way to make day-to-day decisions.

  • Personalized during onboarding.
  • Displayed across the app for simple daily reference.
  • Used in graphs and summaries to show progress clearly.

Added Sugar Score & Sugar Habits

knowsugar includes a score-based and trend-based view of progress. This adds a light layer of motivation while helping users understand patterns over 7-day and 30-day periods.

  • Added Sugar Score: a gamified way to stay engaged and compare progress over time.
  • Sugar Habits: trend views and insights that help users see repeated behaviors and major sugar sources.
  • Shareable reports: snapshots that make progress feel tangible and easy to review.

The knowsugar added sugar search API

The app includes a dedicated added sugar search API designed specifically around sugary foods and drinks. This helps keep search results more relevant to the app’s purpose and creates room for the database to grow over time.

  • Built to expand and cover more sugary foods, drinks, desserts, snacks, sauces, and packaged products.
  • Designed to improve search quality and make logging faster for everyday users.
  • Supports the long-term vision of making added sugar data more useful, searchable, and easier to understand.
